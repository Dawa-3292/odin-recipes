Samosa

Samosa is fried dish popular in south asia and western asia. It is pastry of pudding filling with mostly vegetables.

For the pastory dough:

  • All-purpose flour (maida)
  • Carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt
  • Oil (ghee or vegetable oil)
  • Water

For the filling:

  • Potatoes(3-4 medium size)
  • Green peas
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Green chilies
  • Cumin seeds
  • Coriander seeds

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough:

    Mix 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee, and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Gradually add water and knead into a firm, smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.

  2. Make the Filling:

    Heat oil in a pan, add 1 teaspoon cumin seeds. Add chopped green chilies, peas, and spices (turmeric, garam masala, coriander powder). Stir in boiled and mashed potatoes. Cook for a few minutes, add salt to taste, and let the mixture cool.

  3. Assemble the Samosas:

    Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each into a thin circle, cut in half, shape each half into a cone, fill with the potato mixture, and seal the edges with water.

  4. Fry the Samosas:

    Heat oil in a deep pan. Fry the samosas in batches over medium heat until golden brown. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.
